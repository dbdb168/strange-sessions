The Question

Work is being reimagined.

Leaders are experiencing what it means to work and lead in organisations where intelligence is now distributed, where the capability to build is in the hands of many, and where the roles and functions once thought permanent are now being quietly rewritten.

In our inaugural session, we are inviting leaders whose day jobs now include understanding what this means — for their people, the systems they operate in, and the outcomes they are accountable for.

The Quarterly

A series of convenings where we answer the questions that matter.

The Quarterly runs throughout the year, each one built around a single question worth answering well. We interview practitioners and experts. We seek inspiration from other fields, gather primary evidence, and synthesise what we find into something you can act on. We share what we learn in our sessions.

Discovery

In the weeks before each session, we go deep on one question. We start our research and surface the early patterns that have not yet made it into the mainstream conversation.

Synthesis

We distil what we find into original, proprietary intelligence. A clear-eyed read on what is actually happening, grounded in evidence, shaped by practitioners, and designed to be immediately useful.

Convening

We share our findings with the room, bring in a practitioner who is living the work, and then open it up. Curated breakout groups. Facilitated conversation. The kind of exchange that only happens when you put the right people around the same question.

What You Leave With

Signal, not noise.

Proprietary research

Original findings on how organisations are restructuring around AI. The patterns, the case studies, the failures and the successes. Packaged for you to act on.

A curated room

Leaders who are navigating the same shift. Not AI specialists. People leading teams, departments, and divisions through a transformation they did not train for. The conversations that happen in this room do not happen anywhere else.

A design-led approach

We are three former IDEO leaders. We approach each session as we would a project: challenge-driven, evidence-based, human-centred, and built to generate the kind of insight that changes how you think about the problem.

A private brief

A take-home memo synthesising the research, the key findings, and the discussion. Something concrete you can bring back to your organisation the next morning.
